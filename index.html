Abstract

Reconstructing a photo-realistic, animatable head avatar from only a few images is crucial for scalable digital human creation. 3D Gaussian splatting enables efficient rendering, yet existing Gaussian avatar pipelines largely occupy two ends of the spectrum: optimization-heavy methods that require long videos, and feed-forward methods tailored to a fixed number of inputs, typically one image.

This leaves open the any-shot 1-K regime, where the number of available source images varies across examples and additional inputs should be exploited whenever they provide useful evidence. We present CANVAS, a feed-forward framework that reconstructs unseen identities without per-identity optimization by casting any-shot reconstruction as evidence accumulation on a fixed canonical support.

Given per-image FLAME coefficients and camera poses, CANVAS constructs a multi-resolution UV Gaussian atlas on the FLAME surface, lifts multi-scale foundation features from each image onto shared anchors via geometry-guided projection, and fuses them with a visibility-aware, permutation-invariant aggregation module. For high-fidelity appearance, CANVAS renders latent-feature Gaussians and decodes them with a lightweight refiner. Experiments show that the model outperforms strong one-shot baselines and scales gracefully as more source frames are provided.

Method

Method Highlights

01

Any-shot inputs

CANVAS is designed for the full 1-K regime, so a single feed-forward model can consume one image or a larger set without redesigning the pipeline for each shot count.

02

Canonical FLAME support

A multi-resolution UV Gaussian atlas anchored to FLAME gives the model a stable surface-aligned support for aggregating evidence across views.

03

Visibility-aware aggregation

Geometry-guided lifting and permutation-invariant fusion select and combine complementary observations instead of overfitting to a fixed camera pattern.

04

High-fidelity rendering

Latent-feature Gaussians paired with a lightweight refiner preserve the efficiency of Gaussian splatting while improving appearance quality for animation.
